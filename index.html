User Stories and Acceptance Criteria

Dec 13th, 2013

Launch Academy uses User Stories and Acceptance Criteria to demonstrate how different people mean different things when they say the same things. It clicked for all of us that the “cost of change” is cheap when one has not implemented any code. As soon as code has been implemented the cost of change is “high”. I like how Dan Picket said that user stories and acceptance criteria serve the purpose of “getting a conversation started”. It is not about who “won” the right-wrong contest and blaming each other. I love the word “started” as it means you are opening a door to exchange ideas and explain yourself.

Pair Programming

Dec 13th, 2013

Pair Programming is part of the daily routine at Launch Academy. One thing for sure: Together you can achieve more than on your own. I was really impressed. Clearly I would have never been able to finish the same project on my own without calling an Experience Engineer every 5 minutes to unstuck me. It has been a really good experience to observe you and your peers complement each other and be creative in what to try or where to look next. And for sure you will learn another “trick” from the other person that simplifies your future programming life.

Comraderie is very important in the industry, you cannot survive on your “own”. We need each other as people. “People first” and “opensource” are the keywords of the Ruby Community. And even as they move to other technologies these two will stay.
